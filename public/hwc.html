Health and Wellness Centre(HWC) App & Dashboard Casestudy

Project Overview

The goal was to design a mobile application for Health and Wellness Centre (HWC) doctors and staff in Karnataka to manage Outpatient Department (OPD) services efficiently. The app aimed to streamline patient registration, appointment scheduling, EMR management, telemedicine, health monitoring, inventory management, outreach activities, and provide a dashboard for higher officials.

Role

UX Researcher,

Duration

July/22 - Jan/23 (4 Months)

Target Audicence

Medical Officer,

Userflow

The application's information architecture was redesigned based on user feedback and persona insights. The goal was to ensure that navigation was intuitive, and information was organized in a logical and accessible manner.

UI Design

The UI design focused on clarity, consistency, and usability. Design elements such as color coding for different health programs, clear typography, and intuitive icons were used to enhance user experience and facilitate quick comprehension and navigation.

OPD

Comprehensive Data Capture: User-friendly forms for recording detailed health information across different RMNCH+A categories.

Customizable Alerts and Reminders: Automated reminders for immunizations, antenatal care visits, and other critical health milestones.

Inventory

Session Planning and Management: Tools for scheduling, conducting, and documenting MCHN sessions, including tracking attendance and outcomes.

Educational Resources: Access to multimedia materials and guidelines for delivering effective health and nutrition sessions.

Patient Registration

Personal Health Records: Secure storage and management of patient demographics, medical history, and health progress.

Session History Tracking: A log of all attended health sessions and interventions, facilitating continuity of care.

Outreach activity

Seamless Account Management: Integration with ABHA for simplified registration, tracking of benefits, and accessing health services under the Ayushman Bharat scheme.

Dashboard

Real-Time Data Sync: Integration with the Nikshay portal for TB ACF to ensure prompt reporting, monitoring treatment adherence, and tracking patient outcomes.

Conclusion

The user testing process played a crucial role in refining the Khushi Baby health application. By actively involving ASHA workers and ANMs throughout the design and development phases, we ensured that the application met their specific needs and preferences. The iterative feedback loop allowed us to make informed design decisions, resulting in a user-friendly and effective tool for improving maternal and child health outcomes in underserved communities.
