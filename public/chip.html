Community Health Integrated Platform(CHIP)

An Android application designed for ASHA workers at the village level and ANMs at the sub-center level to manage health records efficiently, reducing the reliance on paper-based tracking methods.

Overview

Khushi Baby is dedicated to improving maternal and child health in underserved communities through innovative technology solutions. To further enhance its impact, my task was to expand the functionality of the Khushi Baby health application. This involved incorporating key health management features and integrating with existing health portals.

Role

UX Researcher,

Duration

July/22 - Jan/23 (4 Months)

Objectives

  • Integrate RMNCH+A (Reproductive, Maternal, Newborn, Child, and Adolescent Health): Provide comprehensive healthcare coverage across various health stages and categories.
  • Implement MCHN (Maternal and Child Health and Nutrition) Sessions: Offer structured support and tracking for maternal and child health and nutrition.
  • Develop a robust Profile Management system: Streamline user data management and access.
  • Incorporate ABHA (Ayushman Bharat Health Account): Facilitate seamless health account management within the app.
  • Integrate TB ACF (Active Case Finding) with the Nikshay portal: Enhance tuberculosis case tracking and management through real-time data integration.

User Research

To ensure that the new features addressed the specific needs and challenges of ASHA workers and ANMs effectively, a comprehensive user research process was undertaken.

Interviews

In-depth interviews were conducted with ASHA workers and ANMs to understand their daily workflows, pain points with existing health management systems, and specific needs related to maternal and child health tracking.

Surveys

Surveys were distributed among a larger group of healthcare workers to gather quantitative data on their preferences, usage patterns, and technological capabilities.

Field Observations

Observations were carried out in the field to observe how healthcare workers interacted with patients, managed health records, and utilized existing tools and technologies.

Key Insights

Need for User-Friendly Interface

Healthcare workers emphasized the importance of a simple and intuitive interface that could be quickly adopted without extensive training. They needed to access and update health records efficiently, even in remote and low-resource settings.

Data Management Challenges

Managing multiple health programs and maintaining accurate health records were significant challenges. There was a demand for a centralized system that could streamline data entry, retrieval, and reporting.

Integration with National Health Initiatives

There was strong interest in integrating with national health portals like Nikshay for seamless data synchronization and reporting, particularly for diseases like tuberculosis.

Persona Development

Based on the insights gathered from user research, personas were developed to represent typical ASHA workers and ANMs. These personas helped in designing features and workflows that catered to their specific needs and preferences.

Key Features

RMNCH+A Integration

Comprehensive Data Capture: User-friendly forms for recording detailed health information across different RMNCH+A categories.

Customizable Alerts and Reminders: Automated reminders for immunizations, antenatal care visits, and other critical health milestones.

MCHN Sessions

Session Planning and Management: Tools for scheduling, conducting, and documenting MCHN sessions, including tracking attendance and outcomes.

Educational Resources: Access to multimedia materials and guidelines for delivering effective health and nutrition sessions.

Profile Management

Personal Health Records: Secure storage and management of patient demographics, medical history, and health progress.

Session History Tracking: A log of all attended health sessions and interventions, facilitating continuity of care.

ABHA Integration

Seamless Account Management: Integration with ABHA for simplified registration, tracking of benefits, and accessing health services under the Ayushman Bharat scheme.

TB ACF and Nikshay Portal Integration

Real-Time Data Sync: Integration with the Nikshay portal for TB ACF to ensure prompt reporting, monitoring treatment adherence, and tracking patient outcomes.

User Testing

User testing sessions were conducted iteratively throughout the design and development phases. ASHA workers and ANMs were involved in testing prototypes and early versions of the application.

Objectives of User Testing

  • Validate Design Decisions: Ensure that the application's features and workflows met user needs and expectations.
  • Identify Usability Issues: Discover any usability challenges or pain points that needed improvement.
  • Gather Feedback for Iterative Refinement: Collect suggestions and preferences from users to refine features and enhance usability.

Testing Scenarios

  • Task-based Testing: Participants were given specific tasks to perform using the application, such as recording health data, scheduling MCHN sessions, and accessing RMNCH+A guidelines.
  • Observational Feedback: Observers noted how users interacted with the interface, where they encountered difficulties, and how they navigated through different features.

Findings and Iterative Improvements

  • Positive Feedback:: Users appreciated the application's user-friendly design and the efficiency it brought to their daily tasks.
  • Usability Challenges: Issues such as unclear navigation paths or overly complex data entry forms were identified and addressed.
  • Feature Requests: Feedback on additional features or enhancements led to iterative improvements that were prioritized based on user needs.

Conclusion

The user testing process played a crucial role in refining the Khushi Baby health application. By actively involving ASHA workers and ANMs throughout the design and development phases, we ensured that the application met their specific needs and preferences. The iterative feedback loop allowed us to make informed design decisions, resulting in a user-friendly and effective tool for improving maternal and child health outcomes in underserved communities.
